Q4: What are the key ideals mentioned in the Preamble of the Constitution of India?
A4: The Preamble of the Constitution of India outlines four key ideals:  
1. **Justice** (social, economic, and political)  
2. **Liberty** (of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship)  
3. **Equality** (of status and opportunity)  
4. **Fraternity** (assuring individual dignity and national unity and integrity).

Q6: According to Article 24, children below what age are prohibited from working in hazardous industries like factories or mines?
A6: Children below the age of 14 years are prohibited from working in factories, mines, or hazardous employment under Article 24 of the Indian Constitution.

Q7: What is the significance of Article 21 in the Indian Constitution?
A7: Article 21 of the Indian Constitution guarantees the **right to life and personal liberty** as a fundamental right. It states that no person can be deprived of their life or liberty except through a legally established procedure. This article is pivotal as it forms the basis for numerous judicial interpretations, including the right to a clean environment, privacy, education, and even the right to die with dignity. It is a cornerstone of individual freedoms and judicial protection against arbitrary state actions.
--------------------------------------------------
Q8: Article 15 prohibits discrimination on certain grounds. However, which groups can the State make special provisions for under this Article?
A8: Under Article 15 of the Indian Constitution, the State can make special provisions for:  
- **Women and children** (Clause 3).  
- **Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes** (Clauses 4, 4A, 5).  
- **Socially and educationally backward classes of citizens** (Clauses 4, 5).  

These provisions aim to address historical inequalities and promote inclusion.
--------------------------------------------------
Q9: Which Article allows Parliament to regulate the right of citizenship and override previous articles on citizenship (Articles 5 to 10)?
A9: Article 11 grants Parliament the authority to regulate citizenship by law, overriding the provisions of Articles 5 to 10. It explicitly states that nothing in those earlier articles limits Parliament’s power to legislate on citizenship acquisition, termination, or related matters.
--------------------------------------------------
Q10: What restrictions can the State impose on the right to freedom of speech under Article 19(2)?
A10: The State can impose reasonable restrictions on freedom of speech under Article 19(2) for:  
- Sovereignty and integrity of India  
- Security of the state  
- Friendly relations with foreign states  
- Public order  
- Decency or morality  
- Contempt of court  
- Defamation  
- Incitement to an offence.  

These restrictions must be proportionate and serve a legitimate public interest.
