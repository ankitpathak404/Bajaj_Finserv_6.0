Q7: What is the significance of Article 21 in the Indian Constitution?
A7: Article 21 of the Indian Constitution deals with the amendment of the Seventh Schedule, which is a list of subjects that are under the jurisdiction of the Union or the States. It allows Parliament to amend the Schedule by adding, varying, or repealing any of its provisions through a law. This article is significant as it gives Parliament the power to modify the distribution of powers between the Union and the States, which is crucial for the functioning of the federal system in India.
