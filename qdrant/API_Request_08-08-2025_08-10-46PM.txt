Q5: What impact will this new policy have on consumers and the global market?
A5: The policy will likely raise prices for consumers in the short term because the 100 % tariff on imported computer chips and semiconductors will increase the cost of devices that rely on those components, and the reduced supply of foreign‑made parts could cause shortages. At the same time, the policy is intended to spur U.S. companies to produce more domestically, which could create new jobs and eventually lower costs if domestic capacity expands. In the global market, the tariff will disrupt existing supply chains, prompting other countries and firms to adjust their sourcing strategies and potentially triggering retaliatory trade measures. The shift may also accelerate the re‑shoring of semiconductor production, reshape trade flows, and increase overall market volatility while encouraging long‑term investment in U.S. manufacturing, such as Apple’s announced $600 billion spend.
