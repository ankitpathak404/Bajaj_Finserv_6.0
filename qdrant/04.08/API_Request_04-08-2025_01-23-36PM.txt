Q7: What is the significance of Article 21 in the Indian Constitution?
A7: Article 21 of the Indian Constitution deals with the amendment of the Seventh Schedule. It gives Parliament the power to amend the Schedule by adding, varying, or repealing any of its provisions. This amendment can be done through a law passed by Parliament. The article also clarifies that such a law shall not be considered an amendment to the Constitution for the purposes of Article 368.
--------------------------------------------------
Q8: Article 15 prohibits discrimination on certain grounds. However, which groups can the State make special provisions for under this Article?
A8: The State can make special provisions for women and children under Article 15, as well as for socially and educationally backward classes of citizens, the Scheduled Castes, and the Scheduled Tribes.
--------------------------------------------------
Q9: Which Article allows Parliament to regulate the right of citizenship and override previous articles on citizenship (Articles 5 to 10)?
A9: Article 11 of the Constitution allows Parliament to regulate the right of citizenship by law, thereby overriding previous articles on citizenship (Articles 5 to 10).
--------------------------------------------------
Q10: What restrictions can the State impose on the right to freedom of speech under Article 19(2)?
A10: Under Article 19(2), the State can impose reasonable restrictions on the right to freedom of speech in the interests of:

- The sovereignty and integrity of India
- The security of the State
- Friendly relations with foreign States
- Public order
- Decency or morality
- Contempt of court
- Defamation
